The Four Stages of Cruelty: The Reward of Cruelty, Detail.

A dog eats Nero’s heart.  This is certainly justice for Tom’s treatment of the dog in the initial plate.

 

Other commentary:

Shesgreen: Next to the pot a smiling dog takes his revenge on Tom’s cruelty to animals by eating his heart (80).

Paulson, Hogarth’s Graphic Works: The complete reversal, in which Nero has become himself the victim, is emphasized by the dog (cf. Pl. 1) who is present, licking his heart (215).

Trusler: A fellow depositing the intestines in a pail, and a dog licking the murderer’s heart, are disgusting and nauseous objects (135).
