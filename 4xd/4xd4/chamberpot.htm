The Four Times of the Day: Night, Detail.

 

A chamberpot is dumped on the freemason, perhaps allowing Hogarth to “piss on” the man and his false morality. The second person in the window confirms that this is a "house of assignation."

 

Other commentary:

Shesgreen: the window above, the contents of a chamber pot fall on his [the freemason’s] head (45).

Uglow: Instead of sweet rain, or country milk, a chamber-pot showers urine which bounces off the lintel straight on the reeling Mason beneath, still in his apron, being guided home by his steward (309). 

Quennell: a woman’s arm empties a chamber-pot from the window of a darkened bedroom, sousing an intoxicated Masonic dignitary (150).

Ireland: The British Xantippe showering her favours from the window upon his [the freemason’s] head, may have its source in that respect which the inmates of such houses as the Rummer Tavern had for the justice of the peace (230-231).
