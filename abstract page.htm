HOGARTH ONLINE

 

by

 

DEBRA TAYLOR BOURDEAU

 

(Under the Direction of Nelson Hilton)

 

ABSTRACT

 

            This dissertation is a web resource that provides commentary on many of William Hogarth’s most well-known engravings series.  It also collects and presents analysis by many important commentators, striving to demonstrate the range of opinion from the eighteenth to the twenty-first century.  Introductory materials discuss the techniques involved in engraving, how the site was assembled and how to read a Hogarth narrative picture series.  Each full-screen version of an engraving contains links to details in the picture to allow viewers to more easily analyze and view commentary on the minute particulars that comprise a Hogarth work.  There are also text-only pages for each plate that give an overview of the work.  All pages have links where appropriate, providing a reader with the ability to more easily observe connections within a plate, between other plates in a series and even across distinct series.
