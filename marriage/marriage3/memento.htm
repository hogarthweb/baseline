Marriage à la Mode, Plate 3, Detail.

 

While seeming a typical memento mori, the skull shows perforations.

 

Other commentary:

Paulson, Hogarth’s Graphic Works: Dr. Misaubin is standing next to a human skull, “the forehead of which has been perforated by the disease in respect to which the party are in consultation” (Stephens, BM Sat.) (271).

Lichtenberg: Upon his table lies a book which has the appearance of being clasped by a somewhat curious skull that had evidently in life been gnawed by the compatriot disease. It is permissible to draw conclusions from such a padlock about the contents of the jewel box; there must be something of the momento mori in it. In that case, it could either contain the mysteries of the doctor's poison-brewing art, or it might be the book of life in which he enters the names and the debts of the blessed cured (106).
