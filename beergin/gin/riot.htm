Gin Lane, Detail.

 

A fight has broken out between two “cripples” who perhaps feign their condition to obtain sympathy and money for gin.

 

Other commentary:

Shesgreen: Behind them a man has taken away a blindfolded cripple’s crutch and uses it as a weapon against him.  The staggering cripple aims a stool at his tormenter; a crowd enjoys the cruel battle (76).

Trusler:  The scene is completed by a quarrel between two drunken medicants, both of whom appear in the character of cripples.  While on of them uses his crutch as a quarter-staff, the other, with great good-will, aims a still, on which he usually sat, at the head of his adversary (130).

Uglow: cripples fight with begging stools and crutches (495).

Paulson, Hogarth’s Graphic Works: Two beggars fight, one swinging the stool he sits on when he begs (210).
