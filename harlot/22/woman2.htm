A Harlot’s Progress, Plate 5, Detail.

 

Here, the dying Moll Hackabout is attended by two physicians.  An additional level of irony is that the one figure consistently devoted to Moll is her noseless, syphilitic servant who demonstrates more humanity than those dedicated to its improvement.  This woman is pictured with Moll throughout her fall, in Drury Lane, Bridewell, and, ultimately at Moll’s funeral.  She is Moll’s true sister in a world of brethren who forsake her.

 

Other commentary:

Shesgreen: Moll’s maid, with one comforting arm around the dying girl, attempts to stop the looting and the turmoil (22).

Uglow: She looks like a huge silent insect in a chrysalis, a shapeless bundle supported by her horrified old maid in a grim burlesque of the death of the Virgin (208).

 Ireland: the maid-servant is entreating them to cease quarrelling, and assist her dying mistress (115).

Quennell: Moll’s agony, propped up in an armchair, is observed by her faithful charwoman (99).

Lichtenberg: She sinks back death-pale into the comforting arms of the woman who had hitherto the care of her soul, and was her faithful companion in the House of Correction, the abominable snub-nose who now suddenly loses all hope of leading her lovely ward, had she only lived and flourished longer to her own advantage, to the gallows (52).
