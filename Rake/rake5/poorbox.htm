Rake’s Progress, Plate 5, Detail.

 

The poor box is surrounded by cobwebs, demonstrating that the church is neglecting its obligations to provide charity.

 

Other commentary:

Shesgreen: “The poors box” is covered with a cobweb. (32).

Paulson, Hogarth’s Graphic Works: The opening of "THE POORS BOX" is covered with a cobweb. (167).

Uglow: The setting was symbolic: the Creed ruined by damp, the Ninth Commandment cracked, the poor box covered by a cobweb (299).

Ireland: so little attention has been paid to the poor’s box, that—it is covered with a cobweb!!! (141).

Dobson: As a further evidence of neglect the slot of the poor-box is covered by a cobweb (Eighteenth-century churches were often allowed to fall into extremely ruinous condition) (42-43).

Lichtenberg: But how do we know that this is the Church of Mary-le-Bone? It is inscribed up there on the gallery: 'This church of St Mary-le-Bone was beautified in the year 1725. Tho. Sice et Tho. Horn Churchwardens. Here thus are the names not only of the church but also of the church- wardens who in the year 1725 so intensely beautified it that in the year 1735 it looked as it did (241).
